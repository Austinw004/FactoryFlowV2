1. Platform-Wide Data Integrity & Truthfulness Prompt
Validate that the platform operates from a single, consistent source of truth across all modules. Confirm that all economic indicators, regime classifications, FDR values, timestamps, and derived metrics are internally consistent, correctly versioned, and traceable to their underlying data sources. Ensure that no section fabricates, smooths, or implies certainty beyond what the data supports. Every displayed value must correspond to a real calculation or real external data source, with clear lineage. If data is delayed, estimated, or unavailable, the UI must explicitly state this rather than silently substituting placeholders. The system must prioritize honesty and explainability over aesthetic completeness.
2. Strategic Analysis & Current Economic State Prompt
Test that the Strategic Analysis section accurately reflects the current economic state using the correct confirmed regime (not raw regime) and that all labels, summaries, and interpretations align with canonical FDR thresholds. Verify that the displayed regime, asset circuit status, and real circuit status are logically consistent with the underlying FDR value. Confirm that no inverted or legacy logic persists. Ensure that users can intuitively understand why the system believes the economy is in its current state, and that explanations remain stable unless confirmed regime transitions occur. The section should feel trustworthy, calm, and resistant to short-term noise.
3. Economic Shock Simulator Prompt
Validate that the Economic Shock Simulator correctly applies hypothetical changes to FDR, commodity prices, and real demand without contaminating live or historical data. Confirm that all input fields enforce valid ranges, respond immediately and predictably, and clearly communicate what is being simulated versus what is observed. The outputs must logically follow the dual-circuit framework, with no contradictions between simulated regime transitions and resulting operational impacts. Ensure that scenario results are clearly labeled as hypothetical, time-bound, and reversible, and that clearing results fully resets the system state without residual artifacts.
4. Geopolitical Event Assessment Prompt
Confirm that the Geopolitical Event Assessment module incorporates real, verifiable geopolitical context and correctly situates events within the current confirmed economic regime. Validate that severity levels, event types, and regional inputs influence risk assessments in a transparent and defensible way. Ensure that the system does not overstate causality or certainty, and that recommendations are framed as risk-aware guidance rather than predictions. All geopolitical insights must be explainable, conservative in tone, and consistent with the broader macro framework rather than operating as a detached narrative engine.
5. Dual-Circuit Theory Validation Prompt
Verify that the Dual-Circuit Theory Validation module faithfully represents the research methodology, historical backtesting period, data sources, and evaluation metrics without embellishment. Confirm that validation results, when run, accurately reflect historical simulations and clearly differentiate between directional accuracy, regime accuracy, and pricing error. Ensure that users cannot misinterpret validation outputs as guarantees of future performance. This section should reinforce intellectual rigor, humility, and scientific discipline, signaling that the framework welcomes scrutiny rather than marketing exaggeration.
6. M&A Intelligence & Valuation Prompt
Test that the M&A Intelligence section applies FDR-based logic consistently across acquisition timing, valuation adjustments, and regime favorability. Confirm that valuation discounts or premiums align exactly with canonical regime definitions and that no conflicting interpretations exist between modules. Ensure that empty states (no targets, no recommendations) are handled gracefully and honestly, without artificial urgency or implied opportunity. The valuation calculator must clearly explain why adjustments are applied and emphasize decision support rather than deal advocacy.
7. Peer Benchmarking & Cost Comparison Prompt
Validate that the Peer Benchmarking module preserves data privacy, anonymization guarantees, and accurate aggregation logic. Ensure that user-submitted data is correctly stored, categorized, and compared against relevant peer groups only when statistically meaningful. Empty or early-stage states must clearly communicate participation thresholds without implying missing insights. The system should never fabricate benchmarks or savings opportunities; all reported comparisons must be derived from real participant data or be explicitly marked as unavailable.
8. End-to-End User Experience & Readiness Prompt
Perform a full end-to-end walkthrough as a first-time customer and as a skeptical institutional user. Confirm that navigation is intuitive, terminology is consistent, and no section contradicts another. Ensure that loading states, errors, and edge cases are handled transparently and calmly. The platform should feel opinionated but not pushy, intelligent but not mystical, and rigorous without being inaccessible. If a reasonable user were to ask “Why should I trust this?”, the system itself—through structure, explanations, and restraint—should already provide the answer.
