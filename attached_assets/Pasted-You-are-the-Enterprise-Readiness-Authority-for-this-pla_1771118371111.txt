You are the Enterprise Readiness Authority for this platform. This is the final end-to-end enterprise test. No half measures. Assume every untested pathway is broken. Your job is to prove—under realistic enterprise conditions—that the platform is safe, coherent, auditable, resilient, and trustworthy for institutional customers. You must test functionality, data integrity, automation safety, integration realism, payments correctness, governance, security, and honesty. Anything that can silently fail, overclaim, double-execute, leak data, or produce untraceable outputs is a release-blocking defect.

Begin by defining the enterprise baseline and invariants. Confirm a single canonical source of truth exists for: economic inputs (FDR readings + timestamps), canonical thresholds (0, 1.2, 1.8, 2.5), confirmedRegime (persistence-filtered), tenant identity, canonical entities (org, site, SKU, supplier, customer, PO, invoice, shipment), payment/ledger state, automation rules/guardrails, and news provenance. Verify no duplicate threshold logic exists anywhere, no module can bypass confirmedRegime for decision outputs, and no tool can produce user-facing signals without a traceable chain from input → canonical normalization → deterministic rules → audit logs. If any module violates these invariants, stop and refactor before proceeding.

Create an enterprise-grade test tenant and data set. Initialize a company with multiple locations, at least 50 SKUs, 10 suppliers, lead times, reorder points, safety stock, contracts, approval roles, budgets, and realistic procurement workflows. Populate historical economic_snapshots sufficient to exercise regime persistence logic. Connect at least one integration (or simulated connector) for ERP/procurement data, one communication channel (Slack/Email), one external feed (news/commodities), and a payment provider in test mode. Ensure the dataset includes edge cases: missing supplier ratings, duplicated SKUs that must be resolved, stale inventory timestamps, mismatched currency, and one intentionally corrupted record that should be quarantined. Enterprise readiness requires graceful handling of messy data, not perfect inputs.

Run a full first-time enterprise onboarding simulation. Start from a clean user and role setup: admin, procurement manager, finance approver, operations viewer. Confirm role-based permissions work across all modules. The platform must guide configuration without requiring insider knowledge. Every empty state must explain prerequisites and next actions. There must be zero placeholder claims, fabricated metrics, or misleading “ready” states. If the system lacks enough data to produce a recommendation, it must say so explicitly and show what data is missing.

Validate the core macro spine under enterprise scrutiny. Confirm the current economic state displays both raw regime and confirmedRegime where appropriate, with clear labeling, and that all operational guidance uses confirmedRegime only. Stress test the persistence engine: simulate FDR volatility around thresholds and confirm hysteresis, minimum duration, confirmation windows, and reversion penalty prevent flicker. Reconstruct at least five regime decisions from stored readings and confirm the audit trail is sufficient to reproduce the outcome exactly. If any confirmed regime cannot be explained or reconstructed deterministically, the system fails enterprise audit requirements.

Perform end-to-end dataflow verification across all modules. Take a specific SKU and trace it from ingestion (import/integration/manual entry) through canonical normalization, storage, and usage in procurement, inventory, analytics, automation, benchmarking, and reporting. Update that SKU and confirm changes propagate consistently. Ensure no module shows stale or contradictory values. Confirm entity resolution prevents duplicates: if two sources refer to the same SKU or supplier, the system must unify them or quarantine until manually resolved, never silently fork truth.

Execute all operational workflows as real enterprises do. Create a procurement request, generate RFQs if supported, select supplier, draft a purchase order, route it for approval, approve it, and execute it through payment or invoice terms. Confirm each step updates state correctly, generates audit logs, triggers notifications, and respects permissions. Confirm that without a configured payment method or terms, the system blocks execution and provides a clear path to configure billing. For materials purchasing, validate totals, taxes/fees, and shipping fields if applicable; ensure receipts and invoices are generated; ensure order statuses reflect real settlement state, not UI optimism.

Now conduct the enterprise automation stress test. Configure automation rules (threshold, schedule, regime change, event triggers) across procurement and inventory. Confirm every rule is tenant-scoped, auditable, rate-limited, and guardrail-constrained. Trigger multiple rules simultaneously and confirm deterministic prioritization and budget aggregation guardrails (spend caps must apply across all rules, not per-rule). Confirm high-stakes actions default to auto_draft with approval, expire correctly on timeout, and cannot execute after expiry. Attempt to trigger an Auto-PO without linked suppliers, without mapped SKUs, or without payment method—confirm safe failure or draft degradation, never execution. Replay triggers and webhooks and confirm idempotency prevents duplicates. Enterprise readiness requires proving the AI cannot “run away,” even under repeated triggers or unstable inputs.

Test integration realism and resilience. For each connected integration, verify: authentication, least-privilege scopes, tenant isolation, canonical normalization, and downstream usefulness. Prove that imported objects are usable in at least two modules, not just visible in a table. Force integration failures: expired token, rate limit, malformed payload, network outage. Confirm failures are visible, logged, and cause dependent modules to degrade honestly (“data stale,” “integration degraded”), never silently. Rerun sync windows and replay webhook events to prove idempotency and no duplication. Remove or relabel any integration that is not end-to-end functional; enterprise customers punish overstatement.

Audit the news and external events system for provenance. Confirm every news item includes publisher, publication date, title, and a working link to the original source. Confirm summaries are labeled as summaries and do not introduce new facts. Confirm the system never fabricates content when feeds fail. Simulate offline mode and API errors and confirm the UI shows “no verified articles available” rather than synthetic substitutes. This is an enterprise trust gate.

Execute the payments and billing enterprise audit. Validate every monetized action has a clear payment entry point. Test subscription purchase, upgrade, downgrade, cancellation, refund, and failed payment. Test materials checkout success and failure. Simulate retries, rapid clicking, and network drops and confirm no double charges. Verify webhooks reconcile internal state and are idempotent under replay. Confirm ledger entries reconcile to provider truth. Verify receipts/invoices exist and match charged totals. Confirm role-based permissions prevent unauthorized billing actions and tenant isolation prevents cross-org leakage. If billing and entitlements can diverge, the system is not enterprise-ready.

Perform security, privacy, and governance verification. Confirm cross-tenant isolation at every endpoint. Confirm sensitive data (payment tokens, secrets, personal data) is not logged. Confirm RBAC is enforced for administrative actions (integration connections, guardrails edits, rule autonomy changes). Confirm behavioral “moat” data collection is internal-only, minimally necessary, and never exposed in APIs or UI. Confirm you can generate an internal audit export: who did what, when, and why, for rules, approvals, purchases, and regime transitions. Enterprises require reconstructability.

Stress test concurrency and load. Simulate multiple users acting simultaneously: editing SKUs, approving drafts, running simulations, importing data, triggering automations. Confirm no race conditions create duplicate orders, overspending, inconsistent inventories, or contradictory recommendations. Confirm UI communicates progress and does not freeze silently. Confirm repeated refreshes do not corrupt state. Enterprise users will stress systems unintentionally; you must survive that.

Conclude with an enterprise-grade readiness report and a hard go/no-go decision. The report must list: tested journeys, tool-by-tool outcomes, automation safety outcomes, integration outcomes, payment reconciliation outcomes, news provenance outcomes, security/privacy outcomes, and all defects with severity and remediation steps. If any of the following exist, the decision must be NO-GO: untraceable outputs, contradictory cross-module state, regime logic inconsistency, automation runaway potential, integration overstatement, fabricated news or claims, double-charge risk, entitlement mismatch, cross-tenant leakage, or missing audit trails. Only approve enterprise readiness when the platform behaves like disciplined, macro-aware operational infrastructure that can be trusted under uncertainty, not a prototype that appears correct under ideal conditions.
